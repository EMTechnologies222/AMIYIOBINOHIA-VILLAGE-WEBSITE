Who We Are

A Village Rooted in
Ancient Igbo Tradition

Amiyi Obilohia is a proud village in Isuikwuato Local Government Area of Abia State, Nigeria. Situated within the Oguduasa clan — one of the three founding lineages of Isuikwuato — our community is an enduring testament to Igbo resilience, kinship, and cultural depth.

A place where the land speaks, the ancestors walk, and the community breathes as one.

Adjacent to Eluoma — the largest town in Isuikwuato — and with the Ogii boundary of Imo State to the south, Amiyi Obilohia occupies a geography that is both beautiful and strategic. Its hills, forest belts, and rich farmlands have sustained its people through generations.

Recent Events & Stories

Security

Community Women Protest Killings by Suspected Gunmen

Women from Amiyi Obilohia shut down the Eke market and gathered at Ama Ukwu Oho Square to protest the killing of two community sons, demanding justice and better security at the Ogii-Imo State boundary.

Faith

St. Silas Methodist Church Hosts Grand Burial Ceremony

The October 2024 three-day burial ceremony for Mrs. Sussana Alakweonye drew mourners from across Nigeria and the diaspora, filling the church to capacity in a powerful display of community bonds.

Development

Abia State Road Project to Open Up Amiyi Obilohia Corridor

A proposed Abia State Government road project identified Amiyi Obilohia and neighbouring communities including Umuebere, Umunnekwu, and Amaba for improved road access to facilitate agricultural transport.
